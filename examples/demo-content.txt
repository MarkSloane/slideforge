Nexus Platform — Q3 Product Launch Review
Presented by Jordan Chen, VP of Engineering

Overview
The Nexus platform launched on July 14 after a 9-month development cycle. This review covers adoption metrics, performance benchmarks, customer feedback, and the Q4 roadmap.

Adoption Metrics
- 12,400 active users in the first 60 days (target was 8,000)
- 340 enterprise accounts onboarded, up 155% from Q2
- 97.3% day-30 retention rate across all cohorts
- Average session duration: 22 minutes (up from 14 minutes on legacy platform)

Performance Benchmarks
Query response time decreased from 1,200ms to 180ms — an 85% improvement.
Concurrent user capacity increased from 5,000 to 45,000 with zero degradation.
Uptime since launch: 99.97%, exceeding the 99.9% SLA target.
Cold start time reduced from 8.2 seconds to 1.1 seconds on the new container architecture.

Infrastructure Cost Savings
The migration to the new architecture reduced monthly compute spend by 38%, from $420K to $260K. Storage costs dropped 52% through intelligent tiering. Total annualized savings: $2.9M.

Customer Feedback Highlights
"The speed improvement is night and day. What used to take our team 45 minutes now finishes in under 3." — Sarah Park, Director of Analytics, Meridian Health

Net Promoter Score: 74 (up from 51 on the legacy platform).
Customer satisfaction rating: 4.6 out of 5.0 across 1,200 survey responses.
Top feature requests: advanced filtering (42%), custom dashboards (38%), API webhooks (31%).

Competitive Landscape
Nexus vs. legacy platform:
- Query speed: 180ms vs. 1,200ms
- Max concurrent users: 45,000 vs. 5,000
- Monthly cost per seat: $18 vs. $32
- Setup time: 15 minutes vs. 2 days

Q4 Roadmap — Key Priorities
1. Advanced filtering engine — addresses the #1 customer request, targeting November release
2. Custom dashboard builder — drag-and-drop interface for self-service analytics
3. API webhook framework — real-time event notifications for enterprise integrations
4. Mobile companion app — iOS and Android, read-only dashboards with push alerts
5. SOC 2 Type II certification — audit begins October 1, targeting Q1 completion

Team Recognition
Engineering velocity increased 40% quarter-over-quarter. The platform team shipped 847 pull requests with a 99.2% CI pass rate. Special recognition to the infrastructure squad for the zero-downtime migration over the July 4th weekend.
